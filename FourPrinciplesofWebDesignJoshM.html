Four Principles of Web Design

BBC Science for Kids (http://www.bbc.co.uk/schools/scienceclips/index_flash.shtml)

Appropriateness: BBC Science for Kids is an online library used for teaching rudimentary science to kids aged 5-11.  The vocabulary and learning content gradually matures throughout the age categories (ages 5-6 being the easiest and ages 10-11 being the hardest). You will not find many long or complex words on this website since kids will learn better with language they can understand. In conclusion, BBC Science for Kids has done a great job with using the appropriate language and content for each age category of kids.

Placement: BBC Science for Kids is good at making kids their target audience. however, they do not make good use of headers, making it difficult to identify which learning category of the website you are at. despite that, this website does an excellent job of dampening the “virtual noise” of the space in between the header and the footer, making it very kid friendly and easy to understand. The footer of the page is small due to the implausibility of it being used by kids, yet effective since parents may still need to attain more information about the website. All of the content of the webpage is above the fold, making it very effective to both kids and their parents.

Consistency: This website does an amazing job with being consistent throughout the branches of the age categories. On every single branch/age group, the style of the background remains the same, the link on the footer remains the same, and the title at the top of the page remains the same for easy navigation.

Usability: Since the target audience of the web page is children aged 5-11, it is extremely important to be able to navigate through the web page with ease. The devs did a great job of this by providing an option at the left side of the page to go straight to the next learning category, go back to the previous learning category, or even to go to a different age group for a bigger challenge. Breadcrumb trails are used heavily due to the effectiveness for the target audience. They make the content intriguing and fun, sparking the thirst for knowledge, and curiosity of the kids to guide them to more interactive learning categories.
